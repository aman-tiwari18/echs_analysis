How the analysis works
Parameters & Methodology

Data. High-value claims (gross claimed > ₹5 lakh) over the last five years, joined from claim_intimation and claim_submission on the intimation ID. Regional figures use the pre-aggregated settlement_stat table.

Claim pipeline. A claim flows: intimation (admission notified) → submission (hospital bills the gross amount) → UTI audit (an approved amount is set; the balance is the deduction) → settlement. A 100% deduction means the audit approved nothing on a multi-lakh bill.

Limitations & Assumptions

• Red flags, not verdicts. Every item is an automated investigative lead, not a confirmed finding. A qualified auditor must verify each case before any action.

• Deduction is not fraud. A high deduction can reflect a legitimate package ceiling, a coding error, or incomplete documentation — it flags where billed value was removed, not why.

• Login codes are identifiers. Hospital login codes (e.g. parkhosg, pol.3325) are used as identifiers only; this report does not assert their real-world hospital identity.

• Data quality. Findings depend on the accuracy of the intimation/submission tables and the UTI deduction codes; systemic gaps (e.g. missing hospital IDs) can both create and hide signals.

• Scope. Analysis covers the last five years only; older claims are out of scope by design.

Why this matters
